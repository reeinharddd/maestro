Maestro

Logo · Attitude Iteration
The brief: Not just a baton. The conductor's commanding presence — the arrogance to stand in front of everyone, the confidence to direct, the strength to correct without fear. This round focuses entirely on visual attitude: boldness, swagger, and the physical authority of the gesture.

01
The Downbeat
"The moment the orchestra snaps to attention"
The conductor's most decisive gesture — the baton coming DOWN. A bold 65° diagonal with a weighted tip, like an exclamation mark. The energy isn't in motion lines; it's in the sudden stop. Silence after impact.

The Arena
"The stage is his. The orchestra is his."
A conductor viewed from behind, facing the orchestra — the iconic "this is my domain" perspective. The figure stands centered, arms wide, baton cutting across the space. The amphitheater/horizon line grounds them. Everything radiates from the figure.

The Crack
"Authority that breaks through everything"
The baton as a bolt of energy — like every force in the room narrows to this single sharp point. A vertical/diagonal slash that cracks through a solid form. Think of a single hairline fracture across a dark slab, or a bolt of light through shadow. The baton doesn't just move — it breaks through.

04
The Sceptre
"The baton as a crown, vertically"
The baton held VERTICAL — not diagonal. This is the conductor at rest but ready, the baton as a sceptre, a ceremonial object of authority. A weighted base (the handle/counterweight) and a tapered ascender. The verticality suggests stature, permanence, command without movement. Think of a scepter, a gavel, a sword planted in the ground.

05
The Sweep
"The gesture that commands an entire room"
The baton as the leading edge of a broad, confident arc — a gesture so wide it encompasses everything. Think of the conductor's arm fully extended, the baton tracing an arc from one horizon to another. This is OPEN ARMS + BATON combined: the welcoming that commands. The arc suggests both embrace and control.

Why this works for "soberbia": The arc says "I own all of this." The baton at the leading edge says "and I'm taking it further." Together they project ambition without aggression, confidence without cruelty.
06
The Stance
"Feet planted. Chest open. Not going anywhere."
The conductor's body as architecture — wide stance, centered, unmovable. This is pure silhouette: grounded legs, open chest, baton held at the ready. Less a logo, more a totem. The proportions are strong, almost architectural. Think of Brancusi — minimal forms that carry immense weight.
Gold · Primary
Dark · Bold
Minimal · Outline
Most "soberbio" of all concepts. A figure so grounded it's almost architectural — wide stance, chest open, baton at the ready. This says "I'm not going anywhere. Watch me work." Ideal for the CLI: the terminal mark of a commander, not a service.
